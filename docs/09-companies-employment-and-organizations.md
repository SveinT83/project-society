# Companies, Employment and Organizations

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Actor types

- **Company:** profit-seeking legal entity.
- **Government:** public authority created by territory and law.
- **Organization:** member-based, normally non-profit entity built around an idea or shared purpose.

Each has independent accounts, assets, obligations, permissions and audit history.

## Companies

Companies may issue shares, appoint a board and manager, hire workers, own assets, borrow, pay dividends, create subsidiaries, merge, be acquired and enter bankruptcy.

The legal entity survives character death or owner inactivity. Owners act through explicit roles; share ownership alone does not bypass company governance.

Accounting is automatic. Players see comprehensible statements, cash flow, liabilities, inventory and risks rather than entering journal entries.

## Employment

Employment may be a standing relationship while work is offered as discrete, claimable jobs. Contracts specify wage rules, permissions, notice, benefits and applicable law.

Employers cannot force an offline player to begin new actions. Unfilled work may remain open, use permitted NPC labour or delay production.

## Organizations

Players may create religions, parties, unions, clubs, professional associations, charities and interest groups. Organizations may:

- define purpose and membership rules
- hold money and property
- collect dues and donations
- build a shared facility
- maintain internal chat, records and votes
- endorse candidates or lobby within law

Organizations do not receive supernatural bonuses merely for declaring a belief. Influence comes from members, assets, communication and lawful or unlawful actions.

## Governance

All entities use explicit roles and permissions. Important changes such as asset sale, share issuance and leadership replacement require the configured decision process and an audit trail.
