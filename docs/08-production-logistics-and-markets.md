# Production, Logistics and Markets

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Production

A production definition names accepted inputs, outputs, equipment, facility capabilities, labour skills, energy, duration, waste and constraints. Definitions are versioned data, not hard-coded controller logic.

Production starts only after inputs and capacity are reserved. Completion consumes inputs and creates outputs exactly once.

Complex goods use meaningful component chains. A car should require many industrial components, but not every real-world bolt. Component depth is justified by distinct suppliers, logistics, quality effects or strategic substitution.

## Facilities

Facilities expose production lines and capacity. Owners choose products, methods, schedules and accepted workers. Automation can repeat organisation production only when stocked, powered and explicitly configured; it does not grant a player unlimited personal labour.

## Logistics

Goods occupy a location. Remote sale does not teleport them. A trade can transfer title before delivery, but fulfilment requires pickup, shipment or a contracted carrier.

Transport capacity, distance, route, fuel, condition, handling and delay affect cost and sometimes quality.

## Employment market

Businesses and governments publish discrete jobs with location, work, duration, wage, qualification requirements and available slots. Players discover jobs at an office or remotely through suitable technology.

A player accepts a job, travels if needed and performs the timed action. Employers may use NPCs for stability, but higher-skilled players should offer superior results.

## Markets

Markets support listings, orders, negotiated contracts and public procurement. Prices are player- and NPC-set within law; the system does not force a universal price.

Transactions record price, tax, ownership, location and settlement. Market history is visible only through permitted public data or purchased services.

## Exploit controls

Validate conservation of goods and money, prevent duplicate completion, distinguish related-party trades, and limit XP/reputation gains from circular self-trading.
