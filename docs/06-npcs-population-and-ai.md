# NPCs, Population and AI

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Purpose

NPCs make sparsely populated regions functional and provide labour, consumption and public continuity. They must not become unbeatable autonomous empires.

## NPC personhood

NPCs are persistent adult persons with identity, household, residence, needs, employment, skills and economic activity. New NPCs enter the world as adults and may immediately seek vocational education or basic work. There is no child-life simulation.

Population creation is a bounded regional process influenced by housing, food, safety, health, employment and public capacity.

## Behaviour tiers

1. **Deterministic routine:** work, consume necessities, travel fixed routes and execute public minimum services.
2. **Policy selection:** choose among configured options using utility scores and constraints.
3. **Constrained AI:** used only for roles where natural-language or contextual judgement adds value.

AI never writes directly to the database, invents commands or changes rules. It proposes a typed command; the application validates cost, authority, law and state before execution. Inputs, output and final command are auditable.

## Competence

NPC skill caps are domain configuration. A committed player must be able to surpass an NPC within a reasonable period. NPCs should have predictable, moderate productivity and conservative risk tolerance.

## Player replacement

Essential roles may begin with NPC officeholders or workers. When a qualified player wins, is hired or lawfully takes the role, the NPC transitions to assistant, reserve or ordinary work without deleting history or breaking services.

NPC fallback may resume after player resignation or sustained inactivity according to transparent rules.

## NPC enterprises

NPCs may operate small firms and essential suppliers when markets would otherwise fail. They use bounded capital, conservative expansion and no hidden subsidies except explicit public-service contracts. They may fail and be acquired.

## Simulation strategy

Active characters use detailed action state. Inactive NPC populations may be updated in regional batches. Promotion to detailed state occurs when a player interacts, observes or materially affects the NPC.
