# Government, Politics, Law and Public Services

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Government levels

Settlements begin as villages with local authority. Villages may join regions or countries, delegating powers according to a constitution while retaining defined local control.

Government roles may include mayor, council member, minister and public-service leadership. NPCs provide conservative temporary officeholders until players qualify and take office.

## Elections and systems

Players may campaign and vote. Laws may define democracy, one-party structures, monarchy, communism or other governance forms, provided constitutional game safety and platform rules are preserved.

Government structure is data and law, not a collection of special-case UI flags. Changes have procedures, effective dates and audit history.

## Powers

Within its jurisdiction, a government may:

- set taxes and budgets
- fund or operate services
- build and maintain infrastructure
- regulate or prohibit goods and activities
- control zoning, weapons, wages or prices
- nationalise or expropriate under applicable law
- establish borders, migration rules and public procurement

The simulator generally permits bad policy and models consequences. Global safety rules prevent account abuse, impossible confiscation paths and evasion of platform policy.

## Public services

Police, fire, health, justice, transport and essential administration require budgets, facilities, equipment and labour. NPC minimum staffing is allowed. Service quality depends on capacity and competence.

## Taxes and budgets

Taxes are calculated and collected automatically. Governments choose rates, bases and spending. Players see understandable forecasts and outcomes rather than filing returns.

## Public records

Property, company, law, election, budget, court and other registers may be public, restricted or secret according to law. Access still requires an in-world channel such as office visit, computer and connectivity.
