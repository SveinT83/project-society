# Education, Research and Technology

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Technology baseline

The world begins at a broadly modern level. Existing concepts such as electricity, vehicles, computers, internet and industrial medicine do not need to be rediscovered.

Research improves methods, efficiency, quality, energy use, safety, recipes and product variants. Advanced facilities such as nuclear plants require suitable knowledge, regulation and qualified staff.

## Schools

There is no primary-school or childhood simulation. Schools provide vocational and higher education.

A school has:

- physical capacity and upkeep
- offered study lines and levels
- teachers and facilities
- entry requirements, fees and funding
- scheduled education actions

Schools can expand or add lines at material and operating cost.

## Teachers and students

Learning output depends on teacher domain skill, teaching skill, facility, curriculum, class capacity and student effort. Education improves learning rate and qualifications; it does not grant instant expert practical XP.

## Research

Companies, governments and universities create research projects with:

- problem and domain
- funding and research-hour target
- facility and knowledge prerequisites
- researcher qualification requirements
- risk, duration and intended outputs

Researchers need relevant practical knowledge, general education and research skill. Research work awards research XP.

## Outputs

Research produces versioned knowledge such as:

- improved production methods
- reduced waste or energy use
- higher achievable quality
- new recipes or product designs
- safer processes

Knowledge may be public, secret, patented or licensed. Patents and licensing are later-stage systems, but knowledge ownership and access must be representable from the start.

## No magical bonuses

Every research benefit modifies a documented process or capability. Avoid global “+5% company output” upgrades without a causal mechanism.
