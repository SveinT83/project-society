# Energy, Utilities and Environment

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Energy

Buildings, factories, mines, transport, computers and public services consume energy. Sources may include hydro, wind, solar, coal, gas, nuclear, biomass and generators.

Generation requires facilities, inputs, labour, maintenance and suitable geography.

## Grid abstraction

The grid models strategic structure without cable-by-cable micromanagement.

- plants connect to transmission capacity
- substations or connection points serve local areas
- connected buildings propagate local service through contiguous developed areas within capacity
- bottlenecks, outages and damage affect supplied zones

Capacity, distance and resilience matter. Players do not wire each apartment individually.

## Utilities

Water, sewage, gas and communication networks may reuse the same service-area abstraction when introduced. Each utility still has distinct capacity, quality and failure consequences.

## Environment

Plots and regions store aggregate pollution and environmental condition. Production, extraction, transport and waste create effects. Regulation, technology, cleanup and time may reduce them.

Pollution can affect health, soil, fish potential, property value and production. It must be causally linked and spatially bounded, not a purely decorative score.

## Renewable resources

Forest and local biological potential recover through periodic bounded updates when habitat supports recovery. Overuse and contamination reduce recovery. The system does not simulate individual organisms.
