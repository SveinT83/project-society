# Crime, Policing, Justice, War and Substances

**Status:** Approved baseline; advanced scope deferred

## Crime

Crime uses timed, abstract actions and probabilistic resolution. It is deep in risk, economics, evidence and social effects, but not graphically depicted.

Crime may include robbery, theft, smuggling, illegal production, corruption, assault, murder and organized networks. Every action declares target, location, prerequisites, duration, exposure and possible evidence.

Outcomes consider skill, condition, equipment, preparation, security, witnesses, police coverage, concurrent offenders and controlled randomness. Formulas must be inspectable and balanced; no single percentage decides every layer.

## Investigation and justice

Crimes generate possible evidence and leads. Investigators perform actions to discover, preserve and connect them. Investigator and offender competence influence probability without guaranteeing either impunity or conviction.

Arrest, detention, trial, sentence and appeal follow applicable local law. Prison time continues in real time at bounded, playable durations. Public officials need authority and audit trails.

## Substances

All substances use a common effect model:

- intended short-term effects
- rebound or compensating cost
- tolerance or dependency where appropriate
- interactions and health risk
- legal status by jurisdiction
- production provenance

Examples may reduce fatigue or hunger temporarily, followed by increased fatigue, hunger or reduced baseline performance. Additional medicines may treat effects but cannot create a cost-free infinite boost loop.

Illegal status creates black-market production, dealing and smuggling opportunities.

## War

War is a future expansion supported by the domain model. Conflict is plot-based and strategic, closer to a board/strategy simulation than action combat.

Military units, equipment, supply, transport, terrain, training and orders resolve attacks on plots. War requires political authority, territorial state and safeguards against destroying the playable world.

## Safety and abuse

Character crime is not permission for harassment, hate, real-world threats or account targeting. Repeated victimisation and spawn trapping require systemic friction, evidence and protective mechanics consistent with the simulation.
