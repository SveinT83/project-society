# Characters, Needs, Health and Skills

**Status:** Approved baseline

## Identity

One account controls one active player character. Characters have persistent identity, location, relationships, assets, obligations, needs, health, skills and history.

## Needs

The initial needs model includes hunger, energy/rest and health. Thirst may be represented separately only if it creates decisions beyond hunger and utilities.

Food varies by nutrition, quality, preparation and safety. Housing equipment such as beds, heating and cooking facilities affects recovery and the ability to prepare goods.

Needs alter performance gradually. Avoid opaque instant failure. The interface must show current state, expected consequences and relevant remedies.

## Health

Use a medium-depth model:

- injuries and common illnesses
- treatment by qualified characters or NPCs
- medicines with intended and adverse effects
- clinics and hospitals with capacity
- reduced work performance and recovery time

Epidemics, detailed diagnoses and advanced insurance products are later expansions unless required by a vertical slice.

## Skills and XP

XP is specific to demonstrated activity. There is no universal character level.

Skill families include practical work, teaching, research, management, public service, investigation, transport, combat and crime. Exact skill taxonomy belongs in versioned domain configuration.

XP awards occur only after meaningful, valid work and must resist trivial repetition, self-dealing and cancelled-action farming. Higher skill can improve speed, yield, precision, safety or quality, but each formula must document which effects apply.

## Education

Education unlocks qualifications and improves learning efficiency. It does not grant mastery equivalent to sustained practice.

## Equipment and effects

Clothing, tools, vehicles, food and substances can modify actions. Effects are server-defined, time-bounded and auditable. No item may grant an unexplained universal percentage bonus.

## Death

Characters do not die of age. They may be killed through world systems.

Death triggers a recovery/respawn process and proportionate losses such as carried goods, interruption, medical cost or temporary incapacity. It does not delete account skills, companies or all long-term property.

Testaments and inactivity succession are approved long-term concepts; exact inactivity periods and inheritance rules remain open.
